# butterfly-effect — Worked Examples

10 examples across 10 domains. Each shows the question, the chain summary, and the non-obvious insight that most analysts miss.

---

## 1. War — Hamas attacks Israel (October 7, 2023)

**Question:** `Hamas attacks Israel — October 7, 2023`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+2h]: Attack → IDF mobilization (0.97)
- Hop 2 [t+6h]: IDF → Brent crude +8.3% via Hormuz risk premium (0.82)
- Hop 3 [t+72h]: IDF → Red Sea shipping reroutes via Houthi response (0.71)
- Hop 4 [t+96h]: Red Sea → Suez Canal traffic -40% (0.85)
- Hop 5 [t+168h]: Suez → EU LNG spot prices +28% (0.63) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 6 [t+720h]: LNG → EU energy inflation re-accelerates (0.58) ⚠️ 4th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The ECB declared victory on inflation in September 2023. The October 7 attack restarted the energy price transmission mechanism via a 6-hop chain. This showed up in Eurostat HICP data in Q1 2024 — 90 days after the event, 6 hops removed from the cause.

## 2. Hurricane — Category 5 makes landfall in Miami

**Question:** `Category 5 hurricane makes landfall in Miami`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+0h]: Storm → infrastructure damage 0.8 severity (0.99)
- Hop 2 [t+1h]: Damage → insurance payout trigger $18B+ (0.95)
- Hop 3 [t+24h]: Insurance → construction material shortage (0.85)
- Hop 4 [t+168h]: Material shortage → 3-state bidding war for lumber/concrete (0.78)
- Hop 5 [t+720h]: Construction demand → mortgage market repricing in coastal zones (0.61) ⚠️ 3rd order

**Non-obvious insight:** The 3rd-order effect isn't the hurricane damage — it's the mortgage market. Climate risk models force lenders to reprice coastal exposure across the entire Southeast, not just Miami. This affects property values in cities that didn't flood.

## 4. Pandemic — Novel pathogen, 30% mortality, 3 cities

**Question:** `Novel pathogen with 30% mortality rate detected in 3 cities`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+7d]: Pathogen → hospital capacity 100% in affected cities (0.95)
- Hop 2 [t+14d]: WHO PHEIC → 47 countries close borders (0.92)
- Hop 3 [t+21d]: Border closures → PPE supply chain collapse (0.88) — 60% manufactured in affected region
- Hop 4 [t+30d]: Supply collapse → healthcare system failure in unaffected countries (0.71) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 5 [t+90d]: Healthcare failure → political instability, emergency powers (0.58) ⚠️ 4th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The 3rd-order effect is healthcare system failure in countries that had zero cases — because their PPE supply chain ran through the outbreak zone. The pandemic exported healthcare fragility before it exported the virus.

**Question:** `Populist candidate wins presidency in major European economy`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+1h]: Election result → currency depreciation 4-8% (0.89)
- Hop 2 [t+24h]: Currency → sovereign bond yield spike (0.84)
- Hop 3 [t+72h]: Bond yields → ECB emergency meeting (0.76)
- Hop 4 [t+168h]: ECB response → contagion to other peripheral economies (0.68) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 5 [t+720h]: Contagion → EU institutional reform pressure (0.54) ⚠️ 4th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The 4th-order effect is EU institutional reform — the election of one populist creates structural pressure to change EU fiscal rules to prevent contagion. The election of one person reshapes the architecture of a continent.

- Hop 3 [t+72h]: China threatens military response — Taiwan Strait tensions (0.82) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 4 [t+168h]: Geopolitical risk → US antitrust review triggered (0.79)

**Non-obvious insight:** The 4th-order winner is Samsung. Every time Nvidia/TSMC merger uncertainty spikes, Samsung's fab expansion business case strengthens. The deal that was supposed to consolidate the semiconductor industry actually accelerates its diversification.

## 7. Climate summit — Major carbon tax announced

**Question:** `G20 announces binding $200/ton carbon tax starting 2026`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+1d]: Carbon tax → fossil fuel company valuations drop 15-25% (0.91)
- Hop 2 [t+7d]: Valuation drop → pension fund rebalancing away from energy (0.85)
- Hop 3 [t+30d]: Pension rebalancing → capital flows to renewables (0.79)
- Hop 4 [t+90d]: Renewable capital → grid infrastructure bottleneck (0.68) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 5 [t+180d]: Grid bottleneck → electricity price spike in transition period (0.61) ⚠️ 4th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The carbon tax designed to reduce emissions causes an electricity price spike in the 4th order — because the grid infrastructure to handle renewable capacity doesn't exist yet. The policy creates the problem it was designed to solve, temporarily.

## 8. Central bank — Fed raises rates 75bps

**Question:** `Fed raises rates 75bps — June 2022`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+2h]: Fed decision → Treasury yield spike 75bps (0.95)
- Hop 2 [t+48h]: Treasury yields → mortgage rate +92bps via MBS repricing (0.87)
- Hop 3 [t+168h]: Mortgage rates → housing starts -247k units (0.72) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 4 [t+720h]: Housing starts → construction sector job losses (0.54) ⚠️ 4th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The 4th-order effect (construction job losses) appears in JOLTS data 30 days after the housing starts data — which itself lags the mortgage rate change by 7 days. Total lag from Fed decision to visible unemployment signal: 37 days. Most analysts attribute the unemployment rise to "the economy" rather than tracing it back to a specific FOMC meeting.

## 9. Supply chain shock — Semiconductor shortage declared

**Question:** `Global semiconductor shortage declared — auto industry impact`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+48h]: Shortage → auto production cuts 25% (0.92)
- Hop 2 [t+72h]: Production cuts → used car prices +48% (0.88)
- Hop 3 [t+120h]: Used car prices → CPI contribution +1.2pp (0.82) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 4 [t+168h]: CPI spike → Fed inflation narrative shifts (0.74) ⚠️ 4th order
- Hop 5 [t+720h]: Fed narrative → rate hike cycle begins (0.68) ⚠️ 5th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The 2021 semiconductor shortage contributed to the 2022 rate hike cycle via a 5-hop chain. The Fed raised rates to fight inflation that was partly caused by a chip shortage in Taiwan. A supply chain event in one industry triggered a monetary policy response that affected every borrower in America.

## 10. Social movement — Mass protest movement goes global

**Question:** `Mass protest movement spreads to 40 countries in 2 weeks`

**Chain summary:**
- Hop 1 [t+1d]: Protests → government emergency response (0.88)
- Hop 2 [t+3d]: Emergency response → social media platform pressure (0.82)
- Hop 3 [t+7d]: Platform pressure → content moderation policy changes (0.74) ⚠️ 3rd order
- Hop 4 [t+14d]: Policy changes → advertiser pullback from platforms (0.68) ⚠️ 4th order
- Hop 5 [t+30d]: Ad revenue drop → platform layoffs and product cuts (0.58) ⚠️ 5th order

**Non-obvious insight:** The 5th-order effect is platform product cuts — features that millions of users depend on get cut because a protest movement in 40 countries triggered an advertiser boycott. The social movement's most lasting impact isn't political change — it's the reshaping of the products billions of people use daily.
